Timeless Diwali Traditions

Diwali is a time to honor age-old traditions that bring families together and celebrate the victory of good over evil. Explore the most cherished customs that make Diwali special.

Key Diwali Traditions

Lighting Diyas

The lighting of earthen lamps (diyas) signifies the triumph of light over darkness. Families decorate their homes with diyas to invite peace and prosperity.

Lakshmi Puja

On the night of Diwali, Lakshmi Puja is performed to worship the goddess of wealth and prosperity. Families pray for happiness and good fortune in the coming year.

Decorating Homes

Homes are adorned with rangoli designs, flowers, and bright lights to welcome guests and the goddess Lakshmi. The colorful decorations are a hallmark of Diwali celebrations.

Sharing Sweets

Exchanging sweets and gifts with friends and family is an essential part of Diwali. It symbolizes the sweetness of life and the bonds of love and friendship.
